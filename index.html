Singapore

Singapore was the first country I landed in during my time in the Navy. The surreal experience of being in this foreign land was pretty intense. I had never really been outside the United States prior to touching foot in Singapore. Also, the total flight time to get to Singapore was roughly 21 hours so it was nice to be on land again.

Malaysia

Malaysia was the first country my ship, USS Sterett (DDG-104), landed in durng my first full deployement. I was was super excited for this port visit considering that I now had a crew of friends to truly explore the port. Needless to say it was a memorable experience.

Yokuska, Japan

Yokuska was the last 7th fleet port on my 2nd deployment. The overall feeling I can remember from this experience is just the sense of hope that home was not too far off in the future. When you're out on the ocean for so long you get into a rut and days run together and home would definitely be a blessing. The subway map pictured below will be my lasting memory from this trip because its a little tricky after a few drinks.

Dubai

Money!Money!Money!Extravagance!Extravagance!Extravagance! Those two words sum up Dubai perfectly. I took the time to actually go to the Burj Khalifa, the tallest building in the world. The Burj Khalifa is a truly mesmerizing structure that many people do not get a chance to experience. Another interesting attraction in Dubai was the Mall of the Emirates. Imagine going skiing in a cournty with 100+ degree weather. You can hit the slopes at the Ski Dubai indoor ski resort inside the Mall of the Emirates.

Phuket, Thailand

It's hard to describe Phuket because it is all a blur full of debauchery. I'll just leave this picture of "James Bond Island". I took a sight seeing tour to this area which was featured in the James Bond film, "The Man with the Golden Gun".

Bali, Indonesia

Bali is a beautiful, inexpensive place to visit. An amazing experince to to close a 10-month deployment before heading back to the States. The most memorable experience was a "little people" wrestling match. It was a choreographed extravaganza that pitted the USA against the Aussies. There was a heavy Aussie crowed with chants of "Aussie!Aussie!Aussie! oi!oi!oi!". My final overseas experience prior to separating from the Navy.
