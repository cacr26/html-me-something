Countries I've Visited During the Navy

Singapore
Malaysia
Yokuska, Japan
Dubai
Phuket, Thailand
Bali, Indonesia

>Singapore

Singapore was the first country I landed in during my time in the Navy.
The surreal experience of being in this foreign land was pretty intense.
I had not been outside the Missouri very much let alone the United States
prior to this experience.

Malaysia

Malaysia was the first county my ship, USS Sterett (DDG-104), landed in
durng my first full deployement. I was was super excited for this experience
considering that I now had a crew of friends to truly explore the port.

Yokuska, Japan

Yokuska was the last 7th fleet port on my 2nd deployment. The overall
feeling I can remember from this experience is just the sense of hope that
home was not to far away. When you're out on the ocean for so long you get
into a rut. The subway map pictured below will always stick out in my mind
from this trip because it is a little trick when you're completely out of
your element.

Dubai

Money!Money!Money!Extravagance!Extravagance!Extravagance! Those two words
sum up Dubai perfectly. I took the time to actually go to the Burj Khalifa, the
tallest building in the world. The Burj Khalifa is a truly mesmerizing structure.
One of the coolest attraction is the Mall of the Emirates. Imagine skiing inside
a mall. You can hit the slopes at the Ski Dubai indoor skir resort inside the Mall
of the Emirates.

Phuket, Thailand

It's hard to describe Phuket because it is all a blur full of debauchery. I'll
just leave this picture.

Bali, Indonesia

Bali is a beautiful, inexpensive place to visit. An amazing experince to
to end a 10-month deployment. The most memorable experience was "little people"
wrestling. It was a choreographed extravaganza that pitted the USA against the
Aussies. This was a heavy Aussie crowed with chants of "Aussie!Aussie!Aussie!
oi!oi!oi!". Another great experience before separating from the Navy.
